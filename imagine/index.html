Your Idea Here

We filled six in an afternoon. There are thousands of us. What would you put on a free riverside site you got to keep for years? Don't overthink it. That was never the hard part. Being asked was.

✏️ add yours below ↓

So what's actually planned?

  • Two ticketed venues, 25 metres from homes.
  • Around eight shows a week, marketed nationally and internationally.
  • Up to 2,250 people onto the Thames Path at once, a footpath about 1.5m beside a two-way cycle lane.
  • The applicants' own planning form says community use is "not relevant."

Built for people with a ticket, not the people who live here.

None of our ideas is the answer, that's the point. A site like this could be a hundred good things: a court, a market, a maker space, a hall by the river. Somewhere you walk into, use, and come back to. A ticketed venue is the opposite: booked months ahead, seen once, no reason to return, locked in for ten years with no say for us. Of everything this could be for the people who live here, they picked the one you can't even use.

Why these, and not just anything?

The developer is choosing to leave this homes site undeveloped for 10+ years, and wants ticketed venues on it in the meantime.

That's a choice. If a site is used temporarily, it should still work for the neighbourhood around it, not just for ticket-holders. So we set a simple test for what actually belongs here.

  • 🌞 daytime use, alive in the day, not just at night
  • 🏠 local value, built for people who live here, not just ticket-holders
  • 🚶 distributed footfall, a steady trickle, not a surge-and-dump
  • 🧱 temporary / removable, fits a "for now" site, packs away clean
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 family-friendly, works for kids and neighbours of all ages
  • 🌙 less late dispersal, fewer midnight crowds into quiet streets

What would you put here?

A sentence is plenty. A scribble is plenty. The developer never asked what this could be, so we're asking each other, and every idea is proof it could be something we'd actually use, not two venues we can't get into.
